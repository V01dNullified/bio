aight so today i saw an insta reel of some guy talking about how atheists are dumb because they cant prove their belief in objective morality. he uses an analogy of 2 people being on an island, each having opposing views on whether murder is good or bad. then he says there is no way to prove whos right if there isn't a religious text or revelation or god that gives them the objective morality.

however, this thats just fallacy of desire, just because there "isn't a way to prove whos right" doesn't mean that god is the correct answer. a circle on a graph not being a function doesn't mean that the actual function is playing hide and seek in the 5th dimension, even if that WOULD explain it.

not only that, no actual atheist believes in objective morality in the first place. theres no law in physics that tells you what is and isn't wrong because morality is a human construct of empathy. the closest to objective morality literally explains how that first analogy of the two people on an island is stupid in the first place.

humans began in close knit tribes to survive. if some random dude named joe in a tribe of 10 decided that he was going to murder someone once a day, how long do you think that tribe would survive? not only that, but joe himself would either get exiled or executed, both meaning his sociopathic genes end with him.

humans evolved to be naturally repulsive of death because death brings more death, if 1/10th of your tribe dies, youre gonna work 50% harder to stay alive. so we evolved to get empathy, to relate, to communicate, and all those things lead to us having a natural, biological sense of morality that is as close to objective morality as it can get.

in the situation of those 2 people on an island, do you really think the guy against murder couldn't draw the easiest conclusion from a path of thinking that follows "if your closest individual got murdered, how would you feel about it. would you want to kill the other person too, or let it be. do you think youre better or worse off in life now that you lost your closest person?"

what justification could someone make for murder is good? i can think of 2, one being law and the other religion itself.

the same guy in that reel said "atheists will say that the general consensus of a society makes the objective morality" (although i have never heard a soul say that), then he draws that society 200 years ago agreed together that slavery was morally good. literally in what situation does this not backfire on your own argument? 200 years ago, virtually everyone was religious, so why did the god given morality not step in there?

of course of course, its the apologist mega-defense statement ill hear, "god works mysterious ways." whats the point of arguing logic with someone who exclusively uses dogma and faith?

it seems every point the guy made in that reel was him sitting on the toilet and thinking "how can i prove my opinions true?" anyone with a lick of scientific understanding would know in an instant that youre to try to DISPROVE your conclusions, not prove it. anyone can prove a factually incorrect statement by just ignoring the evidence against it and making up shit to make it easier to argue for.

oh and to wrap it up, the whole thing was a strawman and false dilemma: no atheist believes in objective morality, and just because there isn't an atheist explanation to objective morality doesn't immediately suggest that there is a god given one.
